## Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a framework for protecting sensitive data processed, stored, and transmitted by SafeHaven. It ensures consistent application of data protection controls across the organization, minimizes risks, and supports compliance with regulations such as HIPAA, PCI-DSS, and SOC 2.

  - **Confidential Business Data**

## Policy Objective
The objectives of this Data Protection Policy are:
1. To safeguard sensitive data against unauthorized access, alteration, or destruction.
2. To ensure compliance with regulatory standards and internal policies.
3. To promote a culture of data security awareness throughout the organization.

---

## Data Protection Principles
SafeHaven adheres to these key data protection principles:
1. **Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency**: Data is collected and processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently.
2. **Data Minimization**: Only the data necessary for specified purposes is collected and stored.
3. **Accuracy**: Data is kept accurate and up to date; inaccuracies are corrected or deleted.
4. **Storage Limitation**: Data is retained only as long as necessary for legal or business needs.
5. **Integrity and Confidentiality**: Appropriate security measures are in place to protect data from unauthorized access or processing.

---

## Data Protection Measures

### 1. Data Encryption
- **At Rest**: Sensitive data is encrypted on servers, databases, and devices.
- **In Transit**: Data transmitted over networks is encrypted using secure protocols (e.g., TLS/SSL).

---

### 2. Access Control
- **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)**: Restricts data access based on roles and responsibilities.
- **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)**: Enforced for accessing critical systems and repositories.

---

### 3. Data Classification
Data is classified into four categories:
1. **Public**: Data intended for public sharing.
2. **Internal**: Data accessible only to employees.
3. **Confidential**: Data requiring limited access within the organization.
4. **Restricted**: Highly sensitive data requiring special handling.

---

### 4. Data Retention and Disposal
- **Retention Periods**: Data is retained based on legal and regulatory requirements.
- **Secure Disposal**: Data no longer required is securely destroyed (e.g., data wiping, physical destruction).

---

### 5. Monitoring and Logging
- **Access Logs**: Tracks access to sensitive data to detect unauthorized attempts.
- **Anomaly Detection**: Systems monitor for unusual activities that may indicate security incidents.

## Data Breach Response
SafeHaven follows these steps in the event of a data breach:
1. **Identify and Contain**: Locate the breach source, contain it, and prevent further access.
2. **Assess Impact**: Evaluate the scope of the breach and affected data.
3. **Notify Affected Parties**: Inform authorities, affected individuals, and partners as required.
4. **Post-Breach Analysis**: Review the incident and implement corrective measures to prevent recurrence.
